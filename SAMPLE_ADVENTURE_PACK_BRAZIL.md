# Sample Adventure Pack: Brazil

A complete walk-through of the Brazil Adventure Pack, used as the worked example any explainer should reference when showing what an Adventure Pack actually contains. Brazil is one of our 46 live country packs and a typical one in shape: 12 missions, real Brazilian content, gated behind Premium or available as a one-off £4.99 purchase.

## What an Adventure Pack is

An Adventure Pack is a country-specific mission set for families travelling (or learning about) that country. Each pack has the same 12 missions in the same shape, but the content of every mission is hand-written for that specific country.

The pack lives at a URL like `/adventure-packs/brazil`. A parent opens it for themselves to read, or hands the phone to a child via their personal Adventure Passport QR code. As the child completes missions, the matching stamps fire into their digital passport.

## The 12 missions, using Brazil as the example

### 1. Map mission 🗺️

A few questions that orient the family geographically. For Brazil:

- Which continent is Brazil on? *South America. Brazil takes up almost half of it.*
- What is the capital of Brazil? *Brasília. Not Rio, not São Paulo. Built from scratch in 4 years and made the capital in 1960, designed in the shape of an airplane when seen from above.*
- What language does Brazil speak? *Portuguese. The only South American country that doesn't speak Spanish.*
- How many countries does Brazil border? (older kids) *10. Every other South American country except Chile and Ecuador.*
- What's the largest river in Brazil? (older kids) *The Amazon. Carries about 20% of all river water on Earth into the ocean.*

Earns: 🗺️ **Map Reader** stamp.

### 2. Language 🗣️

Eight key phrases in Portuguese, with native script, romanisation, and a phonetic pronunciation:

- Hello — *Olá* (oh-LAH)
- Thank you (m/f) — *Obrigado / Obrigada*
- How are you? — *Tudo bem?* (TOO-doo BAYNG)
- Please — *Por favor*
- How much? — *Quanto custa?*
- Delicious! — *Que delícia!*
- Cool / nice — *Beleza* (older kids)
- *Saudade* — a bittersweet longing feeling. Older kids only; there's no English equivalent.

The mission asks the kid to actually try a phrase in the wild. Earns: 🗣️ **Local Lingo** stamp after 3+ phrases attempted (so you can't game it by tapping "done").

### 3. Money 💰

The currency, the symbol, a recommended daily spending challenge.

- Currency: Brazilian Real (BRL, R$)
- Recommended budget: 50 BRL
- Challenge: figure out how to spend it on something local in a day.

Earns: 💰 **Money Changer** stamp.

### 4. Food 🍜

Six dishes worth tracking down. Each has an emoji, a name, and a real description.

- 🫘 **Feijoada**: a black bean stew slow-cooked with smoked pork, sausage and beef. National dish, traditionally Saturday lunch with rice, fried kale and orange slices.
- 🧀 **Pão de queijo**: bouncy, chewy cheese rolls made with cassava flour and Minas cheese. The smell when they come out of the oven is the smell of Brazil.
- 🍇 **Açaí bowl**: frozen blended açaí berry from the Amazon, served like a thick purple sorbet with banana, granola, honey or condensed milk on top.
- 🍗 **Coxinha**: a teardrop-shaped fried pastry stuffed with shredded chicken. Sold in every padaria in the country.
- 🍫 **Brigadeiro**: a small chocolate-fudge truffle rolled in chocolate sprinkles. Made by boiling condensed milk with cocoa and butter. Served at every Brazilian birthday party.
- 🥩 **Picanha churrasco**: prime cut of beef rump cap, rubbed with rock salt, grilled over coals at a churrascaria steakhouse.

Earns: 🍜 **Brave Eater** stamp after 3+ foods tried.

### 5. Geography 🌍

Six places to recognise, beyond the obvious capital.

- ⛰️ **Rio de Janeiro**: the "Marvellous City". Sugarloaf Mountain, Copacabana and Ipanema, Christ the Redeemer, the most famous Carnival on the planet.
- 🏙️ **São Paulo**: biggest city in South America, 22 million in the metro area. Skyscrapers everywhere, the best food in Brazil, a serious graffiti scene.
- 🌳 **The Amazon**: world's largest rainforest, covering 60% of Brazil. 10% of all known species live here.
- 🥁 **Salvador**: the first capital of Brazil, on the north-east coast. The heart of Afro-Brazilian culture, capoeira on every corner.
- 💦 **Iguaçu Falls**: 275 waterfalls on the Argentine border. Wider and taller than Niagara.
- 🐊 **The Pantanal**: world's largest tropical wetland. Better wildlife-spotting than the Amazon — capybaras, jaguars, caimans, macaws.

Earns: 🌍 **Geography Genius** stamp.

### 6. Scavenger hunt 🔎

A list of distinctly-Brazilian things to spot in real life as you travel:

- A yellow Brazilian football shirt
- A samba drum, percussion group, or carnival musician
- The Christ the Redeemer statue (in person or as an image)
- A toucan, scarlet macaw, or bright tropical parrot
- A capybara
- A real Amazon-rainforest tree (or botanical garden palm)
- People playing volleyball or futevôlei on a beach
- A street artist or graffiti mural in São Paulo or Rio
- A baroque colonial church with gold inside (Minas Gerais / Salvador)
- A coconut water vendor with a machete (older kids)
- People playing capoeira in a circle on the street (older kids)
- Bossa nova or samba playing from a beach kiosk (older kids)

Earns: 🔎 **Scavenger Hunter** stamp.

### 7. Animal spotter 🐾

Six animals to look for, with where to actually find them.

- 🐒 **Capuchin monkey**: in Tijuca Forest above Rio, any rainforest reserve, sometimes on city walls.
- 🦜 **Scarlet or blue-and-yellow macaw**: loud, brilliantly-coloured parrots flying in pairs above the Amazon and Pantanal.
- 🐊 **Yacare caiman**: in Pantanal rivers and lakes — usually placid, often dozens in one stretch of bank.
- 🦒 **Capybara**: world's biggest rodent, looks like a chilled-out giant guinea pig. Common in the Pantanal.
- 🐬 **Pink river dolphin** (older kids): in the Amazon. Yes, actually pink. Yes, real.
- 🐆 **Jaguar** (older kids): the Pantanal is the best place on Earth to spot a wild jaguar.

Earns: 🐾 **Animal Spotter** stamp after 3+ animals spotted.

### 8. Senses ✨

Five prompts asking the kid to write down what they actually noticed.

- **Smell**: Charcoal smoke from a churrasco grill, ripe tropical fruit at a market, ocean breeze and sunscreen, fresh hot pão de queijo from a bakery, jungle wetness in the rainforest.
- **Hear**: Samba drums on a Saturday afternoon, Portuguese which sounds like Spanish but isn't, "Tudo bem?", an entire bar screaming "GOOOOL!", parrots in the trees, beach volleyball thumps.
- **Taste**: Cold thick açaí with banana, salty cheese bread, smoky picanha beef, tiny strong "cafezinho" coffees, ice-cold guaraná soda, melting brigadeiro.
- **Feel**: Wet humidity in the rainforest, hot sand in Rio under bare feet, the bass of a passing samba drum in your chest, cool Atlantic spray on a cliff, sticky beach sweat.
- **See**: What's the most incredible thing right in front of you right now? Open prompt for the kid to write.

Earns: ✨ **Sense Seeker** stamp.

### 9. Stories 📖

Five real Brazil stories, each with a question, a fact-rich answer, and (for some) a thinking question at the end.

- **How big is the Amazon, really?** 60% of Brazil. Four times bigger than the second-place rainforest. The river carries 20% of all freshwater that reaches the world's oceans. 40,000 plant species. *Thinking question: if the Amazon is the lungs of the planet, who should be in charge of protecting it?*
- **Why is Carnival such a huge deal?** Started as a Portuguese pre-Lent farewell-to-meat tradition. African and indigenous rhythms turned it into something new. 70,000 people watch dancers in the Rio Sambódromo. 2 million more flood the streets in "blocos". Schools close. Offices close.
- **Why is there a giant statue of Jesus over Rio?** Christ the Redeemer. 30m tall on an 8m base. Built in the 1920s, finished 1931. New Seven Wonders of the World. Gets struck by lightning several times a year — in 2014 it lost a thumb in a storm.
- **Why does Brazil speak Portuguese?** (older kids) The 1494 Treaty of Tordesillas: the Pope drew a line down the Atlantic. East = Portugal, west = Spain. The bulge of Brazil happened to fall on the Portuguese side. Today over 200 million Brazilians speak Portuguese, way more than Portugal itself.
- **How African is Brazil?** (older kids) From the 1500s to 1880s, Brazil received around 4 million enslaved Africans — 40% of the entire Atlantic slave trade. Brazil was the last country in the Americas to abolish slavery, in 1888. Today around 56% of Brazilians identify as Black or mixed-race. Samba, capoeira, candomblé, brigadeiro, much of Brazilian cooking — huge African roots. Salvador in the north-east has the largest community of African descendants in the Americas outside Lagos.

Earns: 📖 **Story Keeper** stamp.

### 10. Family chat ☕

Six conversation cards designed for the dinner table or the car. They don't have right answers — they're prompts to talk about Brazil together.

- Best food you've eaten in Brazil so far?
- Have you tried saying "obrigado" or "obrigada" to a stranger? What did they do?
- Beach, jungle, city, mountain — which has felt the most "Brazil" so far?
- Samba, bossa nova, funk, sertanejo — what music have you heard most often?
- Brazilians are usually really warm to strangers. How has that felt compared to back home?
- (older kids) Brazil is bigger than the entire USA without Alaska. What does "huge" actually feel like here?

Earns: ☕ **Family Chatterbox** stamp.

A word-search puzzle with Brazilian words: BRAZIL, RIO, SAMBA, CARNIVAL, AMAZON, JUNGLE, BEACH, FOOTBALL, JAGUAR, BANANA. Hint: "Brazilian foods, places and things to spot." No dedicated stamp — folds into Pack Complete.

### 12. Tile puzzle

A picture-tile puzzle on a Brazil-themed image. No dedicated stamp — folds into Pack Complete.

The trophy stamp. Awarded when every section above is finished.

## Why we built it this way

Three small reasons that add up to one big one.

- **Specific beats generic**. A kid reading "try a local food" forgets it. A kid reading "try picanha with rock salt" remembers the picanha.
- **Twelve missions, not fifty**. Long enough to feel substantial, short enough to actually finish. Pack Complete should be earnable on a week's trip.
- **Older-kid content lives inside the same pack**. The pack adapts up as the kid does. No need to buy a different version when they hit 11.

The shape (12 missions, this exact order) is the same in every pack, so once a family does Brazil they already know how to do Argentina, Japan, Morocco, anywhere else we ship.

## How an explainer should use this

If a video is explaining what an Adventure Pack contains, walk through Brazil as the worked example. Don't enumerate every mission like a feature list. Pick three or four standouts a parent would actually care about:

- The food list (concrete, kids will eat one of these by accident anyway)
- The animal spotter list (the Pantanal jaguar line lands)
- The Carnival story (kids' attention catches on "schools close, offices close")
- The family chat questions (parents grab onto "what would we ACTUALLY talk about with our kids")

Anchor it in a moment: "Imagine you've landed in São Paulo, the kid's overtired, you've got an hour until dinner. You pull up the pack on your phone, scroll to the Food section, point at the brigadeiro, say 'pick one and we'll find it on the way to the restaurant.' That's it. That's the whole product, just with 12 of those built-in."
