# How Adventure Passport stamps work

A plain-English guide for parents using Jax Family Travels. Stamps are the heartbeat of the Adventure Passport — kids earn them by doing real things on the ground, with you. This document covers every way stamps land in the passport, the controls you have as a parent, and concrete ideas for using stamps as family challenges.

## What is the Adventure Passport?

Every child in your family gets their own digital passport on Jax Family Travels. They scan a QR code on a parent's phone to open their passport in either a read-only view, a guided journaling experience, or a free-form creator mode. The passport collects stamps as they explore, completes Adventure Pack missions, journals what they see, and unlocks larger milestone badges as their travel history grows.

The Adventure Passport is included with Premium membership (£49.99/year), which also includes every Adventure Pack, every premium blog post, and every guide on the site.

## The three ways a kid earns a stamp

Every stamp in the system comes from one of three paths. All three feed into the same passport, and you can mix and match freely.

### 1. Automatically, from finishing an Adventure Pack mission

Adventure Packs are country-specific mission sets — 12 missions per pack, covering map reading, language, local money, food, geography, scavenger hunts, animal spotting, sensory journaling, local stories, and family conversation cards.

When your child completes a section, the matching stamp fires automatically. For three of the stamps (Brave Eater for food, Local Lingo for language, Animal Spotter for animals) the system also requires at least three real interactions before firing — so a kid who taps "done" without actually trying anything doesn't earn the stamp.

By default, auto-fired stamps land as "suggested", waiting for your tick. You can switch that to instant-approve per child if you trust their completion calls.

### 2. You award it yourself, from your parent dashboard

The most flexible path. Open your child's page from /account, hit "Award a stamp", pick any stamp, optionally tie it to a country, and even backdate it if the thing happened last weekend. Manual parent awards always land as fully awarded — they don't go through the approval queue.

This is how you handle anything the app can't see directly: a museum visit, an early morning hike, a brave food moment at dinner. You can also re-award the same stamp if it deserves to repeat (e.g. Brave Traveller for every flight).

### 3. They suggest it themselves, in Creator mode

Older kids in Creator permission mode can suggest stamps for themselves on their own scan-in flow. These always land as "suggested" so you have the final say on every one. Good for kids who want a bit of agency in tracking their own adventure.

## What you control per child

Every child has their own settings, so a 5-year-old and a 12-year-old can live under the same family account with very different experiences.

### Auto-approve toggle

A simple on/off switch per child. When off (default): system-fired stamps land as "suggested" and wait for your approval. When on: they land in the passport instantly. Useful for younger kids whose self-reported completion can be optimistic, and equally useful to turn off when the novelty of "ticking complete" wears thin.

### Permission modes

There are three modes that decide what a child can do when they scan into their passport:

- **View only**: Read-only passport. Nothing to interact with. Safest. Recommended for toddlers and any child you don't want submitting answers.
- **Guided**: Prompted journaling, emoji ratings, and Adventure Pack progress. The default and the right starting point for most families. Kids interact but stay on rails.
- **Creator**: Free-form journal entries plus self-stamp suggestions. Best for older kids ready for a bit of ownership over their record.

You set the mode when you create a child and can change it any time from the child's page.

## Using stamps to set challenges

This is where the system stops being a feature and starts being a family ritual. Pick a stamp, turn it into a challenge for the day or the trip, award it when they pull it off. Stamps stop being something the app gives them and become something they earn from you.

### Real challenges we've used

| Stamp | The challenge |
|---|---|
| Brave Eater 🍜 | "Try three things off the menu you've never had. Picture proof." |
| Culture Spotter 🏛️ | "Find one mural, statue, or temple today and tell me what you think it means." |
| Animal Spotter 🐾 | "Spot five animals on the country's list and tell me about your favourite over dinner." |
| Early Bird 🌅 | "You're in charge of waking everyone up before sunrise tomorrow." |
| Step Champ 👟 | "10,000 steps before bed. Phone in your pocket counting." |
| Story Keeper 📖 | "Read the Story mission in the Adventure Pack, then retell it to grandparents on video." |

After they've done the challenge, open your child's page and award the stamp manually. Backdate it to the day of the challenge if you only get round to logging it later.

### A few principles that work

- **One challenge at a time, not all of them at once.** Stamps stop feeling special if you offer six in one morning.
- **Tie challenges to the place.** "Try three foods" hits different in a Penang night market than at home.
- **Don't over-reward auto-fired stamps.** If the system already gave them a stamp for finishing the Food section, don't also give them Brave Eater again for eating something at dinner. Save manual awards for things the app couldn't see.
- **Use backdating for the magic moments.** When something brave or kind happens, mention out loud "that was definitely a Brave Eater stamp" — then quietly log it later. Lets you stay in the moment.

## The full stamp catalogue

There are 18 stamp types in total, plus a system of larger milestone badges.

### Adventure Pack stamps (11)

Earned by finishing a section of an Adventure Pack. Almost all of these can also be awarded manually if you want to recognise the same achievement offline.

- **🗺️ Map Reader** — Found a country on the map.
- **🗣️ Local Lingo** — Attempted the local language. Requires 3+ phrases used.
- **💰 Money Changer** — Worked out the local money.
- **🍜 Brave Eater** — Tried a local food. Requires 3+ food interactions.
- **🌍 Geography Genius** — Matched the country's places.
- **🔎 Scavenger Hunter** — Spotted things from the scavenger list.
- **🐾 Animal Spotter** — Spotted 3+ animals on the country's animal list.
- **✨ Sense Seeker** — Wrote down what you saw, heard, smelled, tasted, felt.
- **📖 Story Keeper** — Read the country's stories.
- **☕ Family Chatterbox** — Did the family chat cards together.
- **🏆 Pack Complete** — Finished every mission in an Adventure Pack.

### Real-world stamps (7)

Mostly award-it-yourself stamps for things that happen off-screen. The Brave Traveller stamp fires automatically when you log a flight on the family Flight Log.

- **✈️ Brave Traveller** — Took a flight. Awarded automatically when you log the flight.
- **👟 Step Champ** — Walked 10,000+ steps in a day.
- **🗺️ Explorer Day** — Visited 3+ different places in one day.
- **🏛️ Culture Spotter** — Visited a museum, temple, or heritage site.
- **🌿 Nature Lover** — Spent the day outdoors in nature.
- **🌊 Water Adventurer** — Swam, snorkelled, paddled, or surfed.
- **🌅 Early Bird** — Started a travel day before sunrise.

### Milestone badges (computed automatically)

As the passport fills up, your child unlocks larger milestone badges. These aren't stored as individual stamps — they appear automatically when their travel history crosses each threshold.

- **Country count**: 1, 5, 10, 15, 20, 30, and 50 different countries visited. The very first stamp shows the country's actual flag.
- **Brave Eater tiers**: Tried the local food in 5, 10, 15, or 20 different countries.
- **Continent badges**: One per continent + a "X continents" total badge, from 2 through 7.
- **Home country handling**: Whichever country you set as the child's "home" doesn't count toward travel milestones, so kids only get credit for the world beyond their front door.

## Practical workflow for a typical trip

Putting it all together, here's how a typical family might use the stamps system over a week-long trip:

1. **Before you go**: Buy or unlock the country's Adventure Pack. Set each child to their right permission mode. Decide whether you want auto-approve on or off for each of them.
2. **On the road**: Let kids work through the Adventure Pack as you actually do things. The Food, Language, Animal stamps need them to interact 3+ times, so they need to genuinely try.
3. **End of each day**: Open the parent dashboard. Approve any suggested stamps that landed (or save the conversation for the morning if you're shattered). Manually award stamps for the offline moments — the surprise museum visit, the bravery at dinner, the 5am sunrise hike.
4. **Set tomorrow's challenge**: Pick one stamp you'd like them to earn from you. Tell them in plain terms what would unlock it. Make it specific and matched to where you'll be.
5. **End of the trip**: The Pack Complete stamp fires when every mission is done. The country itself joins their visited-countries count, which feeds the milestone badges.

## Why this matters

Stamps are the simplest possible language for "you did something memorable on this trip". They don't grade your child, they don't compare them to other kids, and they don't hand out trophies for tapping a button. They reward attention, bravery, curiosity, and effort — the four things long-term family travel actually rewards in real life.

The system is designed to put you, the parent, at the centre of awarding them. The app handles the boring tracking; you handle the meaning.
